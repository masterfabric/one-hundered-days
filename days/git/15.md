## ğŸ“– Task Explanations

Sometimes you are in the middle of working on something, but you need to suddenly switch to a different task. You don't want to commit your half-finished work, but you also don't want to lose it. This is where `git stash` comes in. It allows you to save your local modifications away and revert the working directory to match the last commit. You can then re-apply your stashed changes later.
