## üìñ Task Explanations

Today is about bringing together your knowledge of asynchronous JavaScript (`Promises`, `async/await`) and the power of TypeScript's basic types. These challenges are designed to mimic real-world scenarios where you'll fetch data from an API and ensure that the data you receive conforms to a specific structure.

By defining interfaces for your data, TypeScript helps you catch errors early and provides excellent autocompletion in your editor. Combining this with `async/await` makes your data fetching logic both robust and readable. The second challenge reinforces your understanding of Promise chaining and how types flow through asynchronous operations.

## üìö Dictionary for Developers

*   **Review of Previous Terms:**
    *   **`Promise`:** An object representing the eventual completion or failure of an asynchronous operation.
    *   **`async/await`:** Syntactic sugar for writing asynchronous code that looks synchronous.
    *   **TypeScript:** A superset of JavaScript that adds static typing.
    *   **Interface (TypeScript):** A way to define the shape of an object, enforcing type-checking.
