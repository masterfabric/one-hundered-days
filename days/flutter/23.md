## üìñ Task Explanations

`Expanded` and `Flexible` are essential widgets for creating responsive layouts that can adapt to different screen sizes. When you wrap a child of a `Row` or `Column` in an `Expanded` widget, it tells Flutter to give that child all the remaining available space.

You can use the `flex` property to control how multiple `Expanded` children share the available space. For example, if you have two `Expanded` children, one with a `flex` of 2 and the other with a `flex` of 1, the first child will take up twice as much space as the second one.

`Flexible` is a more lenient version of `Expanded`. It allows its child to be smaller than the available space, whereas `Expanded` forces its child to fill the space.

## üìö Dictionary for Developers

*   **`Expanded`:** A widget that expands a child of a `Row`, `Column`, or `Flex` so that the child fills the available space.
*   **`Flexible`:** A widget that controls how a child of a `Row`, `Column`, or `Flex` flexes.
