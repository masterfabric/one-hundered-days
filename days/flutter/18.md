## ğŸ“– Task Explanations

In Flutter, widgets are either stateless or stateful. A `StatelessWidget` is a widget that does not have any internal state that can change over time. Its properties are immutable and are passed in from its parent. A stateless widget is only drawn once, when it's first added to the widget tree.

Today, you'll learn how to create your own custom widgets by extending `StatelessWidget`. This is the foundation of building complex UIs in Flutter. You'll learn that the `build()` method is the heart of a widget. It's a function that returns a widget, and it's where you define what your widget looks like by combining other widgets.
